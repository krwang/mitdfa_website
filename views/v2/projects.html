Our project teams are made up of students using human centered design to address a pressing problem in the community like homelessness or healthcare improvement. We identify a problem and then partner with community organizations and individual clients to immerse ourselves into the problem and learn about the issue firsthand. We use the information we gather to continuously reframe our problem to best address the needs of the user.

We then ideate potential solutions to the problem, build these ideas, and test them in the field before we launch them to be deployed in the real world. We strive to build empathic products that directly address our users' needs and can make a difference in how people live their lives.

Boo Boo Buddy

Problem: How can we improve pediatric pain communication in hospitals and homes?
Solution: Boo Boo Buddy is a toy and phone application that allows children to visually communicate their pain and help adults track changes in pain over time.

F.R.E.S.H.

Problem: How can we increase accessibility of food pantries to low-income, working families?
Solution: F.R.E.S.H. containers are distribution machines that store fresh food packages that families can pick up from the F.R.E.S.H. receptacle after the food pantry closes to make them accessible all day and night.
